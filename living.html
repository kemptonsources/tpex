Not all ordinances are done for the living, see proxy ordinances. The ordinances that are done for the living members of the church are called living ordinances.

The Endowment, or blessing, is an ordinance in the temple available for worthy members of the church who are 18 or older. In this ordinance people make five covenants or promises, they are, the Law of Obedience, the Law of Sacrifice, the Law of the Gospel, the Law of Chastity, and the Law of Consecration. In return for keeping these promises church members get blessings from God.

The Sealing ordinance is the marriage ordinance. In the sealing a man and a woman make covenants or promises between themselves and God. This ordinance allows for the man and woman to be sealed in the life to come.
